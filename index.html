GILBERTO ARANGO

MONUMENTAL 

IMAGINARIES

CERAMIC

 

Imagination has no limit. It is the function that, stimulated by knowledge, is renewed in permanence. It is the function that Gilberto Arango exercises when asking himself the reason of the monumentality in the architecture of other latitudes, until finding answer and representation in the natural techniques of the ceramics. With volumes and forms Gilberto Arango, architect by profession and ceramist by conviction, recreates in his ceramic pieces the monumentality of the buildings of worship that, by its form and dimension, acquire symbolic value. His work has led him to confirm that our pre-Columbian ceramics and goldsmiths are the most accomplished and fine on the continent, but in terms of architecture their expression is rudimentary and minimal. With this hypothesis in mind he decided to create ceramics as imaginary buildings that could have existed with the same finesse and detail of the native ceramics and goldsmiths.

The work of Gilberto Arango is a journey through his imagination formed in architecture, but led to plastic expression in ceramic pieces present in this exhibition, where the representations of places of worship, of pre-Hispanic anthropomorphic architectures, of Amazonian malokas, interpret the symbolism of monumental architecture that diverse human groups build around beliefs and cults.

Saúl Alvarez Lara
